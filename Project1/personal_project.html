ABOUT VIETNAM

Vietnam is a country in Southeast Asia, lie in the Eastern edge of mainland in Southeast Asia. Vietnam is known for its beautiful tourist areas, delicious food, history especially Vietnam war and an overall affordable prices for all of your needs!

The most dazzling location on the left is called Golden Bridge, a place in Da Nang, Vietnam. It has a unique design with two large hands spreading wide facing up, as if the hands are lifting up the bridge. Resemble nature hands giving human supports from the ground.

Folklores

Vietnam's history revolves around a lot of myths and folklore. They are often used to teach young children about their origins and ancestors. Most of the famous myths and folklore are “The Fight between Mountain God and Water God” (Sơn Tinh – Thủy Tinh ), “The Vietnamese creation origin” (Lạc Long Quân and Âu Cơ), “The Legend of Lang Lieu” (Sự tích bánh chưng bánh dày), and so much more.

Tradition

Vietnam’s culture has many unique traditions, the most important tradition would be Tet Nguyen Dan - meaning "Festival of the First Day of the Year". The celebrations start to celebrate the arrival of Spring, based on the Vietnamese calendar.

This is an image of a small celebration herein America during Tet in my house. Though the process of making Banh Chung (a type of rice cake we usually eat during the celebration) and other food is long and tiring as I only have me and my mother as the cooker now, the feeling of excitement for Tet is still here in my heart. It feels less exciting than the times I celebrate Tet with my family back in Vietnam, but at least, I still get to celebrate it even when I live all the way here in America.

Cuisines

Every country has its own signature dishes, and Vietnam does too. You may know about famous dishes from Vietnam such as Phở, Bánh Mì, Bánh Chưng, Bánh Dày, Bánh Xèo, and so on. But there are many more dishes that are also very popular in the community.

The left image showed a dish in Ha Noi that I cook with my mother last year, which is also called a Northern Vietnam. This dish is called Bun Thang, a variety of noodle dishes that people eat after Lunar New Year’s parties or people can just go into any restaurant whenever they are craving a hot traditional bowl of noodles soup. Bun Thang consists of chicken broth, thin-cut pork sausage (chả lụa), thin-cut egg, shredded chicken, dried shrimp paste, and chopped vermicelli onion.

Language

Vietnamese maybe not be the hardest or most challenging language to learn, but the way people from different areas say it can confuse a lot of people even the locals.

One easy example I know is the way they pronounce [i] is like [e] in English, or the way many Vietnamese first names that are hard to say for new beginners, such as [Nguyễn], [Hữu], [Đỗ], and so on. Luckily my first name is on the easier part, which consists of only two letters, [Lê], it actually pronounces like LEH but lowers your voice a bit and not LEE like how others usually say it. But since it is easier for them to pronounce so I really don’t have many opinions over that.

Education
Like most countries, Vietnam values a lot in education and becoming successful in life. There are after school tutoring classes that can be taught right by your homeroom teacher, whether it is right at school or in specific locations. There are even designated schools for gifted, especially high schools where everything become very competitive. Different languages are indeed being taught in Vietnam, but the most important and usually only available in school is English. Other languages can be learned through after-school centers, these centers not only teach languages but also math and many more subjects.
